Sholi Coffee

Located in between the green hills of Rwanda is Abateraninkunga Ba Sholi, a coffee cooperative in the Cyeza sector in Muhanga district. By offering high quality green beans to the world, people can enjoy the amazing taste of Rwandan specialty coffee. We process our cherries ourselves at our washing stations, this way we secure the quality of the cherries and offer our members and farmers financial security. At Sholi we empower our farmers and enrich our community. This is reflected in the quality and amazing taste of our coffee.

From coffee nurseries to green coffee

It takes about 3 years for a coffee tree to bear fruit. In the harvest season, farmers from all over the region bring their hand-picked red coffee cherries to the Sholi coffee washing station. Some by truck, while smaller farmers transport by bike or even their heads. Only the reddest and best quality cherries are accepted and farmers receive a fair price. At the washing station the coffee is sorted and all defects are removed. Every cherry consists of two parched coffee beans covered in pulp. This pulp is removed and the parchment is fermented for 12 to 18 hours. The parchment is then washed and soaked. Afterwards, the parchment is moved to appropriate covered tables for sorting. Lastly, it is put on drying tables in the sun for 21 days. The parchment is safely stored until being transported to Kigali. In Kigali, the parchment is being removed and the green coffee beans are ready for export.

Sorting and Selecting the Best Cherries

3 Weeks

After harvesting, the coffee cherries are brought to the washing station where careful selection begins. At this point, we remove any defective or unripe cherries to ensure that only the healthiest, fully ripe cherries move forward in the process. This is a crucial quality control step that greatly influences the final taste of the coffee.

Our team uses both manual and flotation methodsâ€”placing the cherries in water, where the good ones sink and the bad ones float. By removing the unwanted cherries and debris early on, we preserve the integrity of our coffee and set the foundation for a clean, balanced cup. This attention to detail is part of what makes Sholi Coffee stand out.

Sun-Drying the Coffee Cherries

2 Weeks

Once the cherries are sorted, they are spread out on raised drying beds under the sun. This traditional and natural method helps preserve the coffeeâ€™s rich flavor while reducing its moisture content to the ideal level. The cherries are turned regularly to ensure even drying and prevent mold or fermentation.

This process can take up to two weeks, depending on the weather, and requires constant attention. Our team monitors the drying closely to maintain consistent quality. Sun-drying not only enhances the bean's sweetness and complexity but also reflects our commitment to sustainable, low-energy processing methods.

Washing the Coffee Beans

1 Weeks

After pulping the cherries to remove their outer skin, the beans undergo a thorough washing process to remove any remaining mucilage. This step is essential in producing clean, high-quality coffee with bright acidity and vibrant flavors.

At Sholi, we use fresh mountain water in specially designed fermentation tanks to gently separate the beans from any sticky residue. Once fermentation is complete, the beans are carefully rinsed multiple times to ensure purity. This meticulous washing not only enhances the coffeeâ€™s clarity and profile but also reflects our dedication to excellence at every step of production.
