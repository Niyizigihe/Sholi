    Our Story

    Before the establishment of Abateraninkunga ba Sholi coffee cooperative, in our region, coffee was sold to opaque markets and at significantly low prices. Due to this, farmers were starting to lose interest in growing coffee as they couldn’t see the benefits associated with the hard work involved. On the other hand, our country had just started rebuilding following the 1994 genocide against Tutsis.In 2008, thirty (30) brave women who happen to be the founders of today’s cooperative, had bundled their efforts, and started an association whose main services were small scale farming business and money rotation among themselves. In 2010, the association welcomed other members including men and later in 2013, the association became an autonomous cooperative which is now Abateraninkunga ba Sholi coffee cooperative, commonly known as Sholi coffee. “Abateraninkunga” is a Kinyarwanda name that translates “mutual assistance”, and we build on our name to keep our mission of lifting each other towards better livelihoods.

    Empowering Through Community and Equality

    At Sholi, being a cooperative means working together to grow not just coffee, but opportunity. Our members share in the decisions, the work, and the rewards.

    We offer training in farming and financial management to help improve both the quality of our coffee cherries and the well-being of our members. Equality is a core value—we welcome everyone, regardless of gender, disability, or religion. Members benefit from health insurance and a loan system that helps cover essential needs like school fees. Beyond our members, we’ve invested in the wider community by establishing a medical center, a clean water source, and a conference hall—contributions that have earned us respect and gratitude throughout the village.

    Investing in Health and Well-being

    At Sholi Coffee, we understand that good health is the foundation of a strong community. That’s why we established a local health post to provide nearby residents with easier access to essential healthcare services.

    The facility offers basic medical care and health education, reducing the need to travel far for treatment. It’s one of the ways we invest in the well-being of both our members and the surrounding community.

    Promoting Nutrition and Healthy Living

    At Sholi, we go beyond coffee by supporting the health and nutrition of families in our community. Through our community health center, we organize sessions where both cooperative members and non-members learn how to prepare balanced meals for young children to support healthy growth.

    To make a lasting impact, we also provide selected families with chickens to supply eggs for better nutrition and teach them how to start home vegetable gardens. These efforts help families build long-term, sustainable habits for better health and well-being.

    Supporting Livelihoods Through Livestock

    We support our members not only with training and resources, but also by providing cows to help improve their daily lives. These cows offer a regular source of milk, ensuring families have access to essential vitamins and nutrition.

    In addition to better health, the cows also provide manure, which members use for composting and improving their soil. This strengthens both their food security and coffee farming practices, creating a more sustainable and self-reliant community.

    Diversifying Income Through Beekeeping

    To support our members with alternative sources of income during the coffee off-season, Sholi has introduced apiary farming within the community. We began by providing selected members with modern beehives to help them get started in honey production.

    This project not only supports household income but also encourages environmental sustainability through pollination. Our goal is to gradually expand the initiative to reach all members of the cooperative.

    Committed to Sustainable Farming

    At Sholi, we prioritize the health of our environment to ensure the best quality coffee while preserving the earth for future generations. We use proper ground management techniques and a professional irrigation system to prevent soil exhaustion and erosion. Our washing station features a water rotation and cleaning system to minimize waste and protect local water sources.

    In addition to sustainable farming practices, we focus on waste management by separating and reusing biodegradable materials like defects and parchment to create organic fertilizer. We also provide regular training for our farmers and members on environmental preservation and eco-friendly farming methods to maintain a harmonious balance between productivity and nature.

    farmers’ Stories

    Cecile MUSABYIMANA

    Executive Secretary of Sholi cell

    The changes in the neighborhood after the establishment of Sholi cooperative are miraculous. First of all, a lot of people moved away from high-risk zones to a settlement area. Sholi offers a lot of jobs for the community, this creates food security and improves living conditions. Thanks to Sholi cooperative, health insurances and school fees can be paid. Most importantly, they built a health care center which is accessible for all inhabitants of Sholi cell. You can really notice that everyone wants to be a member of Sholi.

    Leonidas KABERA

    Sholi's Largest Farmer

    Before joining Sholi, I did not have market for my crops. Now my farm has become 5 times more productive! My children are able to join university and develop themselves. The best thing about Sholi is their fairness towards their members. When they make extra profit, they make sure it goes back to the farmers. Sholi really cares about its farmers and community.

    Scholastique MUJAWAYEZU

    Farmer

    I used to be a coffee dealer, I would buy coffee from farmers and then sell it. I also had a few trees myself, but because there was no washing station in the region, there wasn’t much profit in it. This all changed when I became a Sholi member. They give me a fair price for my coffee cherries and I was able to buy more land to grow my coffee trees. I saved enough money to help my husband open a little shop in our village. Right now, I even have the ability to employ people our village. Right now, I even have the ability to employ people myself and improve their lives, the way Sholi did for me. It’s an honor to work for Sholi and create better opportunities for my children and grandchildren.

    Joel TWIZEYUMUKIZA

    Farmer

    Becoming a member of Sholi has really improved my life, thanks to Sholi, I was not only able to buy more land, they also provided me with trainings on how to use my land more efficiently. The quality of my coffee plantation improved a lot and I got a better price for them. My financial situation stabilized, I restored my house, got solar panels and permanent livestock.

    Cecile MUKAGANANA

    Founding Member

    I have been involved with Sholi from the beginning, when it was still an association and our main focus wasn’t coffee yet. Thanks to Sholi’s growth, I am now able to provide for myself and my family. My children were able to develop themselves, travel away from the area and have better opportunities. I am proud that my children have learned from my experience of working in a cooperative. While the cooperative is growing, Sholi is still one community who helps each other and has fun together. The relationships within the cooperative are very strong and I am proud to be part of it.
